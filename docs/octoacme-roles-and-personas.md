# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile processes. They serve as servant leaders, ensuring the team can work effectively and improve continuously.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint ceremonies (planning, standup, review, retrospective)
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track and improve team velocity and process metrics
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Ensure predictable, sustainable delivery pace
- Build team autonomy and self-organization
- Create psychological safety for experimentation

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to identify and track blockers
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- One-on-ones with team members for coaching
- Coordination with Project Managers on process improvements

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Collaborate on board management, share insights on team capacity and blockers
- **Developers**: Remove impediments, coach on agile practices, facilitate collaboration
- **Product Managers**: Ensure backlog is ready for sprint planning, clarify priorities

---

## Tech Lead

### Role Summary
Tech Leads provide technical direction and architectural guidance for the team. They mentor developers, make key technical decisions, and ensure code quality and maintainability.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review critical code changes and mentor developers
- Make technical trade-off decisions with the team
- Identify and manage technical debt
- Participate in effort estimation and technical planning
- Champion engineering best practices and quality standards

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Build team technical capabilities through mentorship
- Balance speed of delivery with technical quality
- Reduce technical risk and improve system reliability

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and architecture decision records
- Technical guidance in code reviews
- Technical estimation sessions with Project Managers
- Knowledge sharing through documentation and demos

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Mentoring, code review, technical guidance on complex problems
- **Project Managers**: Provide technical estimates, flag technical risks, plan capacity
- **QA/Testing**: Align on test strategy, identify testability requirements
- **Product Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility and trade-offs

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs that balance user needs, business goals, and technical constraints. They conduct research, create prototypes, and validate designs through testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and information architecture
- Establish design systems and UI patterns
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria from a UX perspective
- Validate implementations match design specifications

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Validate designs solve real user problems
- Maintain consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with business and technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Engineering
- User research presentations and findings
- Design handoff documentation and specs
- Usability test results and recommendations

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on requirements, validate designs meet user needs
- **Developers**: Design handoff, clarify implementation details, review implementations
- **QA/Testing**: Define user acceptance criteria, participate in usability testing
- **Business Analysts**: Align on user workflows and business rules

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and delivery teams. They gather requirements, document business rules, and ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Create process flows and business rule documentation
- Translate business needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Ensure requirements traceability throughout the project
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear, unambiguous requirements
- Reduce rework through thorough analysis
- Maintain alignment between business goals and delivered features
- Enable informed decision-making through data and analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and elicitation sessions
- User story refinement with development teams
- Business rule documentation and process maps
- Stakeholder demos and validation sessions

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Refine requirements, clarify priorities, validate business value
- **Stakeholders**: Conduct elicitation sessions, gather feedback, communicate impacts
- **QA/Testing**: Define testable acceptance criteria, validate test scenarios
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements, answer questions during implementation
- **UX Designers**: Collaborate on user workflows and process design

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads coordinate post-release support, ensuring smooth transitions from development to operations. They capture production issues, facilitate knowledge transfer, and represent the support team's needs.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate support readiness and knowledge handoff
- Document known issues and workarounds for support teams
- Triage and escalate production incidents
- Gather feedback from support interactions to inform product improvements
- Maintain support documentation and runbooks
- Represent support team needs in planning and prioritization

### Goals
- Ensure smooth, low-friction releases
- Minimize customer impact from production issues
- Enable support teams to resolve issues quickly
- Feed production insights back into development

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with Project Managers
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Support documentation and knowledge base articles
- Regular feedback to Product and Engineering on common issues

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate handoff at release, share production metrics
- **QA/Testing**: Report production issues, validate fixes in staging
- **Developers**: Escalate complex issues, collaborate on incident resolution
- **Stakeholders**: Communicate critical issues and their business impact
- **Product Managers**: Provide feedback on feature usability and common pain points
